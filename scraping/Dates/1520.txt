January 19 – King Christian II of Denmark and Norway defeats the Swedes, at Åsunden (Västergötland) in Sweden. The Swedish regent Sten Sture the Younger is mortally wounded in the battle. He is rushed towards Stockholm, in order to lead the fight against the Danes from there, but dies from his wounds on February 3.|@|April 16 – Revolt of the Comuneros: Citizens of Toledo, Spain, Kingdom of Castile opposed to the rule of the County of Flanders-born Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor, rise up when the royal government attempts to unseat radical city councilors.|@|June – Moctezuma II, Aztec ruler of Tenochtitlan, is declared deposed due to his captivity by Conquistador Hernán Cortés. This results in the death of about 400 conquistadors, and some 2,000 of their Indigenous peoples of the Americas allies.
